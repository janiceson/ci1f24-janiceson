Timeline of Events

January 15, 1947

Elizabeth Short's body is discovered in Los Angeles, severed at the waist. The case becomes an immediate media sensation.

January 21, 1947

The Los Angeles Police Department receives an anonymous letter from the supposed killer, taunting the investigation.

January 24, 1947

A handbag and shoe belonging to Elizabeth Short are found in a trash can close to the crime scene, offering new evidence.

January 26, 1947

The police receive another letter containing newspaper clippings and a note that reads, "Here is Dahlia's belongings." The letter is signed by the alleged killer.

February 10, 1947

Additional evidence, including fingerprints and personal items, is found but yields no significant leads, and the case grows colder.

March 14, 1947

Police continue investigating leads and questioning suspects, but they struggle to make any arrests due to lack of concrete evidence.

April 7, 1947

The LAPD releases photos of Elizabeth Short's last known whereabouts in an attempt to garner more information from the public.

September 1947

Despite national interest, the case remains unsolved. The LAPD publicly admits to being no closer to catching the Black Dahlia's killer.
